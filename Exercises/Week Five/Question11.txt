Questions:
(a) What is the Composite Pattern?
(b) Under what conditions would you use a Composite Design Pattern?
(c) What are the four participants of the Composite Design Pattern?

Answers:
(a) The Composite pattern is a pattern which defines a group of objects which are
to be treated as if it were only one instance of the object - it arranges the
objects into a hierarchical tree object.

(b) It should be used when clients ignore the difference between composites of
objects and single objects.

(c) The four participants are:
    the Client
    the Component
    the Composite
    the "Leaf"
